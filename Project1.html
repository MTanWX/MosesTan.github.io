BIG DATA: Current & Near Future Industry Applications

Imagine that as we go about our daily lives — surfing the web, purchasing an item, using a certain brand of shampoo — a trail of information is scattered in our wake; information so voluminous and varied in its content, form and orientation that any average person would find it overwhelmingly difficult to catalog and analyse it without the aid of specialized software applications or machinery. This is the purpose of Big Data analytics, and thanks to recent advances in technology it has become a key transformative tool for organisations to thrive in the modern digital economy.

To begin understanding what Big Data is, we shall break down its verbal composites. A simple working definition of data is: any information that may be electronically stored, processed or transmitted by a machine such as a computer. The key descriptor “Big” is used to signal that that these sets of data are so vast and complex in their features, that traditional software applications normally found on our desktop computers are unable to efficiently process them. Another important feature that makes Big Data challenging to analyse is its strong tendency to grow exponentially larger over time, needing machines and software tools with huge processing power capabilities that can keep up with the speed of its growth.

Finally and most importantly, Big Data should also be understood in terms of its contextual application and potential value as well as digital features. Governments and businesses may use the insights gleaned from Big Data analytics to make better decisions, engage in higher levels of innovation and improve process automation. It is important that organisations are able to extract and exploit the secrets of Big Data instead of just possessing it.
